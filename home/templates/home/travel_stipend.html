What is the travel allowance?

Attending talks, workshops, and meeting other contributors in person is fun and can help you get up to speed faster. As part of your internship, you have a $500 USD travel allowance. You will not be reimbursed for more than $500. You can use the travel allowance to go to a conference, event, or meeting that is relevant to your internship project, or is a Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) conference. Please read the following page carefully before booking any travel to conferences.

Steps to take before and after you travel

  1. Make sure your travel will start within the time that your travel allowance is active.
  2. Confirm with your mentor that the event or conference meets our requirements.
  3. Check whether you need pre-approval from the Outreachy PLC for your travel expenses.
  4. Apply for any scholarships your conference offers to cover travel costs.
  5. Save a flight search screenshot from a widely-recognized airfare comparison site, such as Orbitz, Kayak or Hipmunk.
  6. While on your trip, you may need to save some receipts.
  7. During your event, take notes about what you learned or accomplished, and how it improved your ability to contribute to free and open source software, for your trip report blog post.
  8. Publish your trip report blog post.
  9. Submit a reimbursement request within 2 weeks of the last day of travel. If you submit a reimbursement more than 90 days after the last day of your trip, Conservancy will not refund your travel.

When does the travel have to happen?

The travel allowance is available to you for travel that starts between the day the acceptance results are announced and a year from the beginning of the internship. The one year window is provided to make sure that the travel allowance can be used for travelling to any major yearly event. Currently, we are also typically able to provide extensions for expired travel allowances, upon request. If your unused travel allowance has expired and you have identified a relevant event you could use it for, please request an extension before making plans for the trip by e-mailing outreachy AT SPAMFREE sfconservancy DOT org .

Can the travel allowance be used in parts?

Yes, you can use your travel allowance in parts for multiple trips. While you can combine multiple trips in one reimbursement request, we ask that you submit your reimbursement request within two weeks of the completion of the earliest trip. Note that Conservancy does not reimburse for trips that completed more than 90 days before the request was submitted.

Event requirements

You can use the travel allowance to travel or gain admittance to conferences or meetings, which meet the following two requirements:
  1. '''Strong free and open source software component.''' As the Software Freedom Conservancy is a 501(c)(3) charity in the United States, all activity supported by the travel allowance must forward the organization's mission to promote free and open source software in the public interest. The event you are attending or the material you are presenting needs to have a strong free and open source software component.
  2. '''Your mentor considers it most relevant.''' Each organization that participates in Outreachy has events that are specific to it or the technologies it uses, which might be most relevant for you to attend. Please consult with your mentor about what is the best and most relevant to your project and organization way to use your travel allowance. In some cases, your mentor and you might agree that a generally themed FOSS event close to you is the most relevant use of your travel allowance due to geographical considerations. Do not book your travel before getting a confirmation from your mentor or organization coordinator that they consider it most relevant and will want your travel allowance applied to it.

What types of travel need pre-approval?

Please read Conservancy's policy carefully for full details on what can be covered and what might need additional pre-approval. For example, you will need to get pre-approval for conference registrations exceeding $100 a day, hotel costs exceeding per-diem for your destination, airfare and train tickets purchased less than 14 days in advance, and car rentals. Conservancy's policy is the final authority on what can be covered. Do not book your travel before getting any necessary pre-approval from Outreachy and Conservancy. If a pre-approval is required, it needs to be requested from Outreachy's Project Leadership Committee (PLC), which currently includes Cindy Pallares, Karen Sandler, Tony Sebro, Sage Sharp, and Marina Zhurakhinskaya. ONLY in cases that you need to book travel that doesn't comply with the policy, contact Outreachy organizers for a pre-approval in an e-mail with a subject line "[Round Year]-[Round Start Month]: [Your Last Name]: Travel pre-approval needed". For example, if you participated in the round of Outreachy that started in May 2016 and your last name is Smith, you would use the following subject: "2016-05: Smith: Travel pre-approval needed".

What can the travel allowance cover?

Generally, the travel allowance can cover transportation, lodging, visa, meal, internet, or admission expenses for activities through which you can improve your understanding of your project, organization, and free and open source software by working with or learning from other people. You can use your travel allowance to go to an office of the organization you are working with, an event where other contributors from your organization will be present, a place where you can work with your mentor in person, an event where you will present about your project, or any generally themed FOSS event.

Airfare

For air travel, please be sure to comply with Conservancy's low fare policy and be sure to save a screenshot or "print to file" version of your airfare search on a widely-recognized airfare comparison site, such as Orbitz, Kayak or Hipmunk. Such low fare documentation is required for Conservancy to do an airfare reimbursement.

Saving receipts

You will need to save the receipts for travel to get to your destination, for hotel, and for conference registration. Note that you won't need to provide receipts for meals and other incidental expenses like local transportation that are part of the Meals and Incidental Expenses per diem amount

What if my expenses are over $500?

Many conferences offer scholarships and reduced or waived admission fees for low-income participants or diversity scholarships. Please investigate and use these in addition to relying on the travel allowance. Outreachy will not cover more than $500. If you wish to use scholarships or travel funding, most events require you to apply several months before the conference. Outreachy is not involved in travel funding for any particular conference or event, so you will need to work with the event organizer to submit a travel funding application. If in doubt, email the event organizers and ask when the deadline for submitting travel funding requests is. Each event will have a different travel policy than the Software Freedom Conservancy travel policy. You may need to provide additional documentation (such as meal receipts). Some events may not require you to write a blog post about the event. Please check with the event organizer for what documentation is needed for their travel reimbursements.

How do I get reimbursed for the travel allowance by Conservancy?

Unless you were told otherwise, Conservancy is the organization that will reimburse your travel. Around the time that their second internship payment is sent, each intern receives an e-mail with a ticket number they can use to request reimbursement for their travel stipend. The e-mail has the intern's round, name, and the phrase "travel reimbursement request" in its subject line. Interns should reply to that email, including their project mentor on Cc, with their reimbursement request. Once the email is received by Conservancy's request ticket system, it will automatically send an email to the Outreachy organizers. If you are ready to request your reimbursement early in your internship round, before the ticket is created for you, you can contact Outreachy organizers for us to ask Conservancy to create such ticket for you. The email body should be in the following form: 
Greetings,

I traveled to $CONFERENCE during $START-DATE to $END-DATE. My mentor on $PROJECT-NAME is Cc'ed and, in a prior discussion, has approved this travel for me as relevant.

My trip blog post can be found at: $URL

I'm requesting reimbursement for $TOTAL, which is broken down into:

Flight: $
Per diem: $
Conference Fee: $
...

The following receipts are attached for my reimbursement:

file1 - airfare search screenshot
file2 - ...

I have not included receipts for things that fall under the Meals and Incidental Expenses per diem amount described on https://sfconservancy.org/projects/policies/conservancy-travel-policy.html#meals-and-incidental-expenses

[INCLUDE PAYMENT INFORMATION OR WHETHER IT IS OK TO USE THE SAME PAYMENT INFORMATION FROM YOUR INTERNSHIP PAYMENTS.]
Within 30 days of your e-mail, Conservancy will send you the reimbursement or inform you if it requires additional information.

How do I get reimbursed for the travel allowance by my organization?

If a participating organization has a process for reimbursing travelling expenses of its contributors, it might choose to do so itself for its Outreachy interns. In that case the organization will be the custodian of your travel allowance, and you will be notified about it. You will need to follow the reimbursement procedures of that organization, and should ask your mentor or coordinator to let you know what they are. {% endblock %}
